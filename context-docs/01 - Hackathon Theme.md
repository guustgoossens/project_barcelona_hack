# 01 — Hackathon Theme

## Project Barcelona — April 15-16, 2026

- 24-hour build, teams of max 3, science fair–style judging
- ~80 engineers, top European builders
- Venue: Itnig (Barcelona). Afterparty: Norrsken House (beach)
- Organized by: Project Europe, with Kibo, Acurio, Itnig
- Sponsors: CoreWeave, Anthropic, Lovable

---

## Tracks — Real Briefs (confirmed at opening ceremony)

### 🎯 OUR TRACK — Clay: "Signal over Noise"
- **Provocation**: Build a tool that helps you find and connect with people who will change your life.
- **What they want**: A system that finds incredible, non-obvious candidates and generates outreach that makes a specific, compelling case for why that person should care.
- **Key judge signal (Yash, Head of GTME Ecosystem)**: Extreme creativity is the #1 criterion. Use case doesn't have to be recruiting — romantic matching, co-founder search, etc. all valid.
- **Judged on**: JD chosen, people found, quality of scores + methodology, outreach copy quality, creativity bonus (Lovable/Claude Code layer, actually sending emails, anything unexpected)
- **Prize**: 12 months Clay Launch Plan + London Summit + Sculpt SF + Alpha Forge seats + GTM Bootcamp
- **Resources**: Clay access + 10,000 credits (top-ups available), Clay University docs

### Biorce: "The Library of Babel — AI Edition"
- **Provocation**: Can AI agents bring order to the chaos of clinical documentation?
- **What they want**: Intelligent document organisation and retrieval — metadata, versioning, linkage automated to reduce manual overhead and human error.
- **Teaser**: "47 related documents, 48 hours before your audit. Find them all, flag what's changed."
- **Prize**: €250 Amazon voucher/person + private lunch + day with AI/ML team ← small prize
- **Why we skipped**: Prize is weak. Brief is document management (not patient recruitment as assumed). No voice angle.

### Cala: "The Wolf of Wall Street — AI version"
- **Provocation**: Everyone says AI will beat the markets. Nobody's proved it with real data.
- **What they want**: Agent that beats S&P 500 buy-and-hold over past year, with reasoning. Live leaderboard.
- **Prize**: €10,000 Cala credits (1 year) + travel grant to European hack
- **Resources**: Unlimited Cala API credits, MCP

### Preply: "Measuring and visualizing language-learning progression"
- **Provocation**: How do you turn complex learning signals into clear, trustworthy evidence of improvement?
- **What they want**: Explainable, evidence-based concept proving language proficiency improvement in numbers, simple enough to trust.
- **Prize**: $2,000 USD cash ← only significant cash track prize
- **Resources**: Dataset of anonymized lesson transcripts (5 lessons, 2 students, 6+ month interval)

### THEKER: "Robotic perception under uncertainty"
- **Provocation**: Robots don't fail because they can't detect objects — they fail when visual signals degrade.
- **What they want**: Maximize correct object understanding as visual inputs become increasingly noisy.
- **Prize**: All-expenses Barcelona visit + day with engineering team
- **Resources**: Synthetic dataset (RGB images, object labels, multiple degradation levels). Python 3.10+ only.
- **Why we skipped**: Pure ML/CV, no product angle.

### Vexor: "The Last Human Industry"
- **Provocation**: Will this person pay, and what should I do about it?
- **What they want**: Agent that predicts payment likelihood + recommends action + explains reasoning, on unseen cases.
- **Prize**: F1 Grand Prix Barcelona tickets (June 2026) for each team member
- **Resources**: ~200 anonymised real European debt cases, Anthropic/OpenAI API keys

---

## Grand Jury Prize: €5,000

Goes to the best submission across ALL tracks. Judged by generalists — they see every demo.

**What wins**: universal problem understood in 10s, visceral live demo, technical depth visible at a glance, emotional or intellectual shock.

**Our angle**: TRIBE v2 brain activation heatmap changing in real time = the clip nobody forgets.

---

## Judging Format

Science fair — judges walk between tables. You pitch to everyone who stops. The Grand Jury picks the one demo they can't stop thinking about that evening.
